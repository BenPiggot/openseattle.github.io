CodeAcross Seattle

February 22, 2014 / 10 a.m. – 6 p.m.

A one-day event focused on open data & civic technology in Seattle

What we are doing

CodeAcross Seattle, February 21, is a full day of events for incubating civic innovation in our city. We’re bringing together local governments, businesses, schools, community, as well as social service organizations, media outlets, foundations, and the public to collaborate on civic innovation that will help our residents and improve our region.

What's happening?

Open Data day

CodeAcross Seattle coincides with International Open Data Day!

Open Data Day is a gathering of citizens in cities around the world to write applications, liberate data, create visualizations and publish analyses using open public data to show support for and encourage the adoption open data policies by the world's local, regional and national governments.

Learn more at opendataday.org

CodeAcross

CodeAcross 2014 is a weekend of events taking place in cities around the world. There will be events hosted simultaneously in dozens of cities all around the globe on the weekend of February 21-23, 2014. The goal of CodeAcross is to activate the Code for America network and inspire residents everywhere to get actively involved in their community.

This year's theme for CodeAcross: Beyond Transparency. Let’s take the open government movement one step further, by focusing on not only making data open, but actionable. Join us to push the ball forward in Seattle, WA.

Who Should Get Involved?: Urbanists, Civic Hackers, Government Staff, Developers, Designers, etc. — anyone with the passion to make their city better.

Learn more about CodeAcross.
